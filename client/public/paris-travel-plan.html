Itinerary

Day 1: Arrival & Parisian Charm

Morning

Arrive at Charles de Gaulle Airport (CDG), private transfer to Hotel Plaza Athénée. Check in and settle into your luxurious accommodations.

Afternoon

Stroll along the Champs-Élysées, window shop at designer boutiques, and enjoy a coffee at a classic Parisian café.

Evening

Fine dining experience at Le Jules Verne, a Michelin-starred restaurant located on the second floor of the Eiffel Tower.

Consider booking Le Jules Verne well in advance, as reservations are highly sought after.

Day 2: Art & History Immersion

Morning

Private guided tour of the Louvre Museum, focusing on key masterpieces and hidden gems. Prioritize seeing the Mona Lisa, Venus de Milo, and Winged Victory of Samothrace.

Afternoon

Explore the Latin Quarter, visit the Sorbonne University, and wander through the charming streets filled with bookstores and cafes.

Evening

Attend a performance at the Opéra Garnier, a stunning architectural masterpiece. Enjoy a pre-theater dinner at a nearby bistro.

Wear comfortable shoes for extensive walking throughout the day.

Day 3: Versailles & Gardens

Morning

Day trip to the Palace of Versailles. Guided tour of the palace, including the Hall of Mirrors and the royal apartments.

Afternoon

Explore the extensive gardens of Versailles, take a boat ride on the Grand Canal, and visit the Petit Trianon and Marie Antoinette's Estate.

Evening

Return to Paris. Romantic dinner cruise on the Seine River, enjoying the illuminated landmarks.

Pre-book transportation and tickets to Versailles to avoid long lines.

Day 4: Montmartre & Artistic Flair

Morning

Explore Montmartre, visit the Sacré-Cœur Basilica, and enjoy the panoramic views of Paris.

Afternoon

Wander through Place du Tertre, where artists create and sell their work. Get your portrait sketched.

Evening

Experience the Moulin Rouge show, a classic Parisian cabaret. Enjoy dinner at a traditional French restaurant in Montmartre.

Day 5: Food & Shopping Delights

Morning

Cooking class at Le Cordon Bleu, learning to prepare classic French dishes.

Afternoon

Luxury shopping experience at Le Bon Marché, a high-end department store. Explore the Marais district for unique boutiques and vintage shops.

Evening

Wine tasting at a prestigious cellar, followed by dinner at a gourmet restaurant in the Saint-Germain-des-Prés area.

Day 6: Hidden Gems & Local Experiences

Morning

Explore the Canal Saint-Martin area, known for its trendy cafes, boutiques, and street art.

Afternoon

Visit the Musée Rodin, showcasing the works of Auguste Rodin, including "The Thinker" and "The Kiss."

Evening

Enjoy a jazz performance at a famous jazz club in Saint-Germain-des-Prés.

Day 7: Departure

Morning

Enjoy a leisurely breakfast at your hotel.

Afternoon

Last-minute souvenir shopping. Private transfer to Charles de Gaulle Airport (CDG) for your departure.

Key Attractions

Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower is a wrought-iron lattice tower on the Champ de Mars in Paris, France. It is named after the engineer Gustave Eiffel, whose company designed and built the tower.

Best Time to Visit: Early morning or late evening to avoid crowds.

Admission Fees: €17.10 - €26.80 depending on access options.

Louvre Museum

The Louvre Museum is the world's largest art museum and a historic monument in Paris, France. A central landmark of the city, it is located on the Right Bank of the Seine in the 1st arrondissement.

Best Time to Visit: Wednesday or Friday evenings for smaller crowds.

Admission Fees: €17 per person.

Notre-Dame Cathedral

Notre-Dame Cathedral is a medieval Catholic cathedral on the Île de la Cité in the fourth arrondissement of Paris, France. The cathedral is considered one of the finest examples of French Gothic architecture.

Best Time to Visit: Currently under reconstruction, but the surrounding area is worth visiting.

Admission Fees: Free (access to the grounds).

Palace of Versailles

The Palace of Versailles was the principal royal residence of France from 1682, under Louis XIV, until the start of the French Revolution in 1789, under Louis XVI.

Best Time to Visit: Weekdays in the off-season.

Admission Fees: €18-€20.

Gardens of Versailles

The Gardens of Versailles occupy part of what was once the Domaine royal de Versailles, the royal demesne of the château of Versailles.

Best Time to Visit: Spring or Summer for the full blooming effect.

Admission Fees: Included with Palace ticket or separate admission on some days.

Etiquette

General Etiquette

Greetings: Start with "Bonjour" when entering a shop or interacting with someone.

Dress Code: Parisians tend to dress stylishly. Consider dressing smartly, especially for dining.

Dining Rules

Table Manners: Keep your hands above the table during meals. Bread is often placed directly on the table, not on a plate.

Tipping: Tipping is not mandatory, as a service charge is usually included. However, you can leave a small additional tip for exceptional service.

Cultural Behavior

Public Displays of Affection: Generally accepted, but avoid excessive displays.

Noise Levels: Keep your voice down in public spaces.

Transportation Guidelines

Public Transportation: Be mindful of your belongings. Offer your seat to elderly or pregnant passengers.

Sidewalks: Walk on the right side of the sidewalk.

Travel Tips

Money & Payments

Euro (€) is the currency. Credit cards are widely accepted, but it's good to have some cash for smaller establishments and markets.

Current Exchange Rate: Approximately €1 = $1.10 (as of November 2023 - please check current rates).

Transportation

Paris Visite pass is a good option for unlimited travel on the Metro, bus, and RER within specified zones.

Consider using the Batobus, a hop-on-hop-off boat service on the Seine River, for scenic transportation.

Connectivity

Free WiFi is available in many cafes, restaurants, and public spaces.

Consider purchasing a local SIM card for mobile data if you plan to use your phone extensively.

Packing Essentials

Pack layers for unpredictable weather. Comfortable walking shoes are a must.

Include a waterproof jacket or umbrella for potential rain. A stylish scarf can add warmth and flair.

Cultural Tips

Learn a few basic French phrases to show respect and enhance your interactions with locals.

Be prepared for a slower pace of service in restaurants and cafes. Relax and enjoy the experience.
